Neural Networks: A Simple Explanation

Neural networks work similarly to the human brain, utilizing interconnected nodes (neurons) to process and interpret data. Just like our brain identifies objects through visual cues, neural networks learn from data patterns to perform tasks such as recognizing images or predicting outcomes.

  • Input Layer: Receives the initial data input.
  • Hidden Layers: Analyzes data patterns and refines understanding through adjustments.
  • Output Layer: Provides the final result or prediction.

Training involves repeatedly presenting examples and refining internal parameters (weights), allowing the model to improve over time, enhancing its accuracy and predictive capabilities.
